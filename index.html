The Beginning

Born and raised in a small town outside Chicago, I grew up surrounded by my parents' eclectic record collectionâeverything from Motown classics to experimental jazz. At age 7, I started piano lessons, but it wasn't until I found my mom's old acoustic guitar in the attic that something clicked.

By 15, I was writing my own songs, performing at local cafes and uploading covers to YouTube. What started as weekend hobby quickly became an obsession that would shape my entire life path.

2016

First EP & Breakthrough

After moving to Brooklyn and working night shifts at a 24-hour diner, I recorded my first EP, "Midnight Reflections," in a converted storage unit with equipment borrowed from friends. The project was entirely self-funded and self-produced.

Against all odds, the lead single caught the attention of several music blogs and eventually made its way to college radio. When the track "Silver Moments" went viral after being featured in a popular web series, everything changed overnight.

Milestone: First EP "Midnight Reflections" reaches #3 on indie charts
2018

Debut Album & First Tour

With a small but dedicated fanbase and the support of an independent label, I recorded my debut album "Electric Dreams in Analog Rooms." The fusion of electronic elements with stripped-back acoustic arrangements created a sound that critics described as "hauntingly familiar yet refreshingly innovative."

The album cycle included my first national tour, opening for established acts and eventually headlining smaller venues. Those nights on the roadâsleeping on couches, meeting fans in person, and refining my live performanceâtaught me more about music and connection than any studio session ever could.

Sophomore Album & Critical Acclaim

During the global pandemic, I found myself isolated in a cabin by the lake, armed with just my instruments and a simple recording setup. What emerged was "Whispers Through Walls"âa deeply introspective album that explored themes of isolation, technology, and finding human connection in an increasingly disconnected world.

The album resonated strongly during a time when everyone was experiencing their own version of isolation. The song "Digital Distance" was nominated for a Grammy, and the album was featured on several "Best of the Year" lists despite minimal traditional promotion.

Milestone: "Digital Distance" receives Grammy nomination for Song of the Year
2022

Collaboration Album & World Tour

My third album "Collective Frequencies" represented a dramatic shift in my creative process. Instead of working in isolation, I collaborated with artists from around the world, incorporating influences from jazz, classical, electronic, and traditional folk music from various cultures.

The subsequent world tour brought these collaborations to life on stage with rotating guest performers in each city. The tour culminated in a special live-streamed concert from Tokyo featuring all 15 collaborators performing together for the first and only time.
